🦠 Detectable Skin Diseases

Our AI model can classify the following 7 skin disease categories from the HAM10000 dataset

⚠️ Critical

Melanoma

Code: mel • 1,113 images

A dangerous form of skin cancer arising from melanocytes. Early detection is crucial — 5-year survival exceeds 99% when caught early.

🏥 Requires immediate medical evaluation
🔴 High

Basal Cell Carcinoma

Code: bcc • 514 images

The most common type of skin cancer. Rarely metastasizes but can cause local tissue damage if untreated.

👨‍⚕️ Consult dermatologist promptly
🟠 Moderate

Actinic Keratoses

Code: akiec • 327 images

Pre-cancerous lesions caused by chronic UV exposure. Can progress to squamous cell carcinoma if left untreated.

☀️ Prevent further sun damage
🟢 Low

Benign Keratosis

Code: bkl • 1,099 images

Includes seborrheic keratoses and solar lentigo. Generally harmless growths that do not require treatment.

✅ Monitor for changes
🟢 Low

Dermatofibroma

Code: df • 115 images

Common benign skin growth, often appears as a small, hard bump. Usually requires no treatment unless symptomatic.

✅ Usually harmless
🟢 Low

Melanocytic Nevi

Code: nv • 6,705 images

Common moles formed by clusters of melanocytes. Most are benign but monitor for asymmetry, border, or color changes (ABCDE rule).

👁️ Monitor with ABCDE rule
🔵 Low-Moderate

Vascular Lesions

Code: vasc • 142 images

Includes cherry angiomas and angiokeratomas. Blood vessel-related lesions that are generally benign.

✅ Typically benign
